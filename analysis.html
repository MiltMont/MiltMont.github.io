Real Analysis

We can regard real analysis as the study of a special subset of \( End_{Set}(\mathbb{R}) \), which we denote by \( C^\infty( \mathbb{R}) \) and whose elements are "continuously differentiable" or "smooth"  functions. 

This is a rather loose description, but it encapsulates the goal of developing the concepts of continuity, convergence, differentiation, and integration.
