Real Analysis

What's the point?

To put it bluntly, the goal of studying real analysis is to understand the set of functions

$$End_{Set}(\mathbb{R})$$

or more precisely, the subset of continuously differentiable functions:

$$C^\infty(\mathbb{R}) \subset C^k(\mathbb{R}) \subset End_{set}( \mathbb{R} ) $$

Now, why would we want to do that? 

To fully grasp the importance of the maps \( f: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R} \) we first need to understand that the set  of real numbers represents a beautiful intelectual achievement.
